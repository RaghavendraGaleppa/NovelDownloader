# Chapter 66: Thunder Within the Organs

Zhou Qing quietly stored away the animal hide, convinced it concealed some hidden opportunity. Earlier, he had shown a similar hide to Zhang Jingxiu, but even he couldn't identify its origin.

Moreover, how had this hide ended up deep in the mountain forests, inside the belly of that crimson python? The serpent's stomach had also contained remnants of a fierce tiger.

Was the hide something the python had swallowed itself, or had it come from the tiger it devoured?

Thus, Zhou Qing and his two companions—along with their rooster—continued their journey through these desolate, uninhabited mountain woods.
