# Chapter 97: Martial Dao True Meaning and the Manifestation of the Physique Transformation

Li Xian felt a refreshing coolness rise from his Dantian, surging first toward the top of his head and the soles of his feet. The exhaustion from yesterday was swept away, replaced by an indescribable sense of comfort.

Internal Qi grew in abundance...

Flesh, muscles, bones, and skin cheered in unison.

Since mastering the "Qi Circulation Cycle," a martial artist's perception of their own body has become incredibly sharp, allowing for "Internal Viewing." While called viewing, it is more of a deep internal sensation.

Internal Qi converged within the Dantian.

Like a lake.

Before this, Li Xian’s Qi Lake was roughly a zhang wide and a zhang long. Of course, this was merely a quantification based on sensation.

After reaching the Pinnacle of Mastery...

The Qi Lake expanded by two zhang, and a ripple of True Meaning appeared upon its surface. Waves surged, and ripples spread endlessly.

"Martial Dao True Meaning?"

"What is this thing?"

Li Xian focused his mind to observe, but his limited knowledge prevented him from understanding its origin. There was no one to ask, either. Even a figure like Pang Long did not possess martial arts attainments at the "Pinnacle of Mastery."

Qi strengthens the spirit and replenishes blood and energy.

Li Xian had never felt so refreshed and energetic.

He felt like shouting at the sky, comparing his voice to the eagles soaring above, and testing his agility against the monkeys in the trees.

"Exhilarating!"

Li Xian’s voice shook the surroundings.

Snow slid off the roofs of the surrounding houses, startling a flock of birds.

"I worked so hard to seal my blood and close my pores, accumulating this martial prowess and storing this Qi."

"There are 'Crab Treasures' in these mountains. They are neither difficult to boil nor hard to stew. They are the most suitable resource for me. Now, my feud with the Mountain Wind Stronghold is to the death."

"If I wish to enjoy the mountain's treasures in peace, I must eliminate all enemies in the mountains!"

"Rather than waiting to be beaten passively, this time... I shall take the initiative to seek trouble!"

Lu Xuefeng deliberately took a long detour to avoid revealing the location of the Mountain Wind Stronghold. It took him a whole day and night of walking before he returned to the stronghold.

By the time he reached the foot of the mountain, it was noon.

He saw that the stronghold's defenses were lax, the watchtowers near and far were empty, and the patrolling guards had no spirit, merely going through the motions. He couldn't help but frown and shouted with Qi:

"Audacious!"

His voice was like a thunderclap, a roar of a tiger or a dragon. The surrounding trees shook, and snow fell in a哗啦啦 (huālālā) cascade. In an instant, the slackers in the stronghold were terrified out of their wits.

"Ouch!"

"It's the Fifth Leader! You've finally returned!"

The crowd gathered around him, though the number wasn't large, totaling only thirty or forty people.

"What is going on? Why are there so few people?"

"And you lot are so slack! Are you looking to die?"

With that, Lu Xuefeng swept his sleeve. His Internal Qi was mighty, and the palm wind swept out, knocking several people tumbling.

"Mercy, Fifth Leader! Please have mercy!"

The bandits knelt down and kowtowed.

"Where are the other brothers?" Seeing the state of the stronghold, Lu Xuefeng knew the situation was bad.

"Fifth Leader, we'll tell you the truth, please don't be angry." A slightly bolder bandit said tremblingly, "They... they ran away."

"Ran away?!"

Lu Xuefeng frowned.

The Mountain Wind Stronghold had, at most, three hundred and forty people. Ordinary small bandits had no names recorded in the roster, so it was true that they could just run away.

"Y-Yes," the bandit said. "Since the Third Leader died, the stronghold has been in a panic. People are saying all sorts of things—some say it's haunted, others say we offended a big shot."

"That's why many brothers, terrified in their hearts, left without a word... they all ran away."

"But don't worry, Fifth Leader. Those of us who stayed are loyal and brave, willing to live and die for the stronghold. We won't run."

Bandits are naturally untrustworthy and unrighteous, all of them bloodthirsty and cruel. Having suffered such a severe defeat at the hands of an external enemy, with the Fourth and Third Leaders falling one after another, they dared not stay any longer.

The tree hadn't even fallen, but the squirrels had already scattered.

"You... you said Third Brother died?" Lu Xuefeng asked in shock.

"Yes, died horribly. His belly was slashed open, and his intestines spilled all over the ground," the bandit said with a clicking tongue.

"In that case, does that mean we get an extra share of fine food?" Lu Xuefeng felt a moment of joy, but after the brief happiness, he felt even more uneasy.

Third Brother Peng Fox was the stronghold's mastermind. With him by his side, the stronghold had developed well, providing immense help.

Deep inside the stronghold.

Lu Xuefeng returned to the Hall of Brotherhood.

He saw the corpse of the Third Leader, Peng Fox, merely covered by a straw mat. There wasn't even a coffin, and the valuable fox fur cloak he wore had long been stolen.

Lu Xuefeng looked at Third Brother's tragic state and felt no sadness. Instead, he suddenly thought:

"These ungrateful wretches. The way they treated Third Brother, they will treat me the same if something happens to me. If I fall, won't they just strip me and throw me away?"

Anger flared up. He suddenly struck out a palm horizontally. With a "slap," he hit a bandit.

The bandit's neck snapped, and he fell straight down, dead.

"You dare?!" Lu Xuefeng said nothing more and struck out several palms in a row. Each palm was ruthless. In an instant, five more bandits died by his hand.

The remaining bandits all fled in terror.

"Guard Third Brother's corpse well," Lu Xuefeng snorted coldly and walked straight down the mountain.

He had intended to return to the stronghold and discuss with Third Brother how to take revenge and defeat the enemy, and how to show their might and fame. He never expected Third Brother to have been killed as well, leaving his plans in ruins.

He thought back to the contents of the letter.

The Fourth Leader, Niu Liang, led the stronghold's men out to seek revenge, but their fate is unknown. It is likely they were defeated, which means the enemy also has forces.

Relying on the trash of the stronghold to gather men and fight again would be no different from another defeat. It would be better to act alone, scout the enemy's movements, and break them from within. That would be more advantageous.

At worst...
